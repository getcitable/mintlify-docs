Returns the full body text of one content item, plus its title, SEO metadata, and status. This is how you pull a finished piece out of Citable and into your own website, CMS, or workflow.

Use this when:
- You're publishing a Citable-drafted article on your own site and need the actual text.
- You have a content id (from list_content, create_content_draft, emit_content, or get_action_with_children) and want what's inside it.
- You want to quote, repurpose, or adapt an existing piece.

Skip when:
- You want a list of content (use list_content — it returns metadata + ids; pair it with this tool).
- You want a quality score for the text (use score_content).
- You want citation performance for a posted piece (use get_attribution_report).

Response (structuredContent): \{ contentId, title, contentType, status, format ('html' or 'markdown'), body, wordCount, characterCount, seo: \{ metaTitle, metaDescription, slug \}, postedUrl, postedAt, scheduledFor, createdAt, updatedAt \}. The text response is the body itself, ready to use. Blog bodies are usually HTML (they come from the rich-text editor); social bodies are plain text or markdown — check 'format'.
