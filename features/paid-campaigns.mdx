Some of the highest-intent prompts in your category are also the hardest to win organically. Competitors with deeper content libraries or older domains often hold the top citation slot. Paid placements let you skip the wait — and Citable's recommendations make sure you're buying the right prompts at the right time.

## What's supported today

ChatGPT Ads is the first AI ad surface Citable supports end to end. You can:

- Identify prompt clusters where you're losing organically
- Launch a campaign, ad group, and ads against those prompts
- Pause, archive, or update placements as performance changes
- See branded vs non-branded performance side by side
- Compare paid citation lift against your organic baseline

Perplexity, Gemini, Grok, and Copilot will plug in as each engine opens advertising. The strategy and measurement work the same way — only the surface changes.

## How Citable picks targets

Most paid ad strategies pick targets the wrong way: chase competitor keywords from Google Ads and hope they translate. Citable does the opposite — it starts from your AI visibility scan and surfaces prompts where:

- You're under-performing on share of voice
- Competitors are *also* spending paid budget in adjacent search channels
- The prompt has high enough intent to justify paid cost

The double signal — Citable's scan plus paid competitor activity — is the filter. Anything passing both is a strong target. You see the proposals before any spend goes live, and you decide what to launch.

## Where paid fits in the strategy

Paid is one of [five action types](/concepts/action-types) Citable recommends, not the default. For most brands, content and technical fixes are the higher-ROI plays in the first quarter. Paid earns its place when:

- You need to win a prompt cluster *now* and can't wait six months for content to compound
- You're testing a new positioning angle and want a fast read on whether it lands
- A competitor is dominating a prompt you have a strong organic case to win — paid buys you the time to build it

## Measurement

Every paid placement is measured the same way as organic content: 30 days after it goes live, Citable computes the citation rate and share of voice lift attributable to that placement. You see whether the campaign moved the needle or not — and you can compare paid to organic in the same dashboard.

This is the part most ad platforms can't show you. Paid AI placements are too new for industry benchmarks; Citable produces the benchmarks from your own data over time.
